Diana Wynne Jones

1934 - 2011

Diana Wynne Jones was a novelist who wrote fantasy and speculative fiction for children and young adults. She is most notably known for Howl's Moving Castle and her Chrestomanci books.

  • 1956 - Graduated from St. Anne's College in Oxford where she attended lectures by C.S. Lewis and J.R.R. Tolkien.
  • 1970 - Published first novel Changeover with Macmillan.
  • 1978 - Won the Guardian Children's Fiction Prize for Charmed Life
  • 1999 - Received the Karl Edward Wagner Award for her significant impact on fantasy
  • 2006 - Received honorary D.Litt from the University of Bristol
  • 2007 - Received the World Fantasy Award for Life Achievement

Jones passed away on March 26, 2011 after being diagnosed with lung cancer in summer of 2009. She had three sons and five grandchildren. She was loved by many for her fantastical tales.
